About


I'm originally from Wayland, a suburban town outside of Boston, MA. (There's actually a display server protocol named after it.) I attended the local high school and then headed to Medford, where I graduated from Tufts University with a B.S. in biology and computer science in February of 2020.

I am interested in the intersections of human health and technology — namely computational biology. I hope to conduct research that will allow me to use software-based approaches to address issues of human health.

In my spare time, I shoot film and digital photography. You can also find me reading feminist and critical race theory on my phone. Recently, I also practice tai chi.

Some of my more general interests include: vegetarian cooking, non-English music, film analysis and review, and German Shepherd dogs.
